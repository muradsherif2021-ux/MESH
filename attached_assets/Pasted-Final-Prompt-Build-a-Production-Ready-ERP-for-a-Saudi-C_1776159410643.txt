Final Prompt — Build a Production-Ready ERP for a Saudi Customs Clearance Business
Arabic-First | RTL-First | IFRS 15 Agent Model | VAT/ZATCA | Double-Entry Accounting

I want you to design and implement a modern, production-ready, scalable ERP system for a Saudi customs clearance business.

This is not a simple CRUD app, not just invoicing, and not a lightweight bookkeeping tool.
It must be a real ERP built around a correct accounting engine, operational simplicity, auditability, and future scalability.

The system is for a customs clearance company that acts as an AGENT, not as a principal.

==================================================
1) BUSINESS MODEL — MANDATORY
==================================================

The business works as follows:

- Customers authorize the company to complete customs clearance procedures on their behalf.
- The company pays multiple charges on behalf of customers in order to release shipments.
- These charges include:
  - customs duties paid to ZATCA / customs
  - freight / shipping-related charges
  - port charges
  - loading / unloading charges
  - shipping agent charges
  - field advances / driver advances / miscellaneous on-behalf expenses

After clearance is completed, the company issues an invoice to the customer containing:
- amounts paid on behalf of the customer
- customs clearance fees (true company revenue)
- administrative/service fees if applicable
- VAT only on the company’s own taxable service fees, not on pass-through amounts

Core accounting truth:
Amounts paid on behalf of the customer are NOT company revenue and NOT company operating expense.
They are recoverable pass-through amounts and must be treated as temporary recoverable balances until invoiced.

True revenue includes only:
- clearance fees
- company-owned service/admin fees

VAT rule:
- The company is VAT-registered in Saudi Arabia
- VAT is applied only to clearance/service fees when applicable
- VAT is NOT applied to pass-through amounts

==================================================
2) ACCOUNTING PRINCIPLE — MANDATORY
==================================================

The system must be designed under:
- IFRS 15
- Agent Model
- Double-Entry Accounting
- VAT / ZATCA-ready principles
- Full auditability

Do NOT:
- treat customs duties as company revenue
- treat freight / port / shipping agent costs as company revenue
- treat pass-through costs as company operating expenses
- treat VAT as revenue
- build financial statements directly from operational tables

All financial reports must be based only on POSTED JOURNAL ENTRIES.

==================================================
3) FINAL DESIGN DECISION — MANDATORY
==================================================

The system must be built around:

Pooled Cost Recording + Invoice-Time Allocation + Draft/Final Invoice Lifecycle

Meaning:

A) Costs are first recorded as pooled/unallocated cost sources
Examples:
- shipping agent trip charges
- shipping agent additional fees
- customs payments
- field advances / miscellaneous pooled costs

B) These cost sources may serve MULTIPLE customers / shipments
So they must NOT be assigned to a single customer by default.

C) Actual allocation happens later at invoice time
When creating the invoice, the user allocates the exact share belonging to that customer.

D) Allocation is not necessarily equal
Equal allocation must NOT be the primary logic.
Actual allocation is the main design.
Equal allocation can exist only as an optional helper tool.

Create a unified cost source concept.

Examples of cost source types:

==================================================
7) INVOICE ALLOCATION DESIGN — MANDATORY
==================================================

At invoice time, the user must be able to allocate specific amounts from available cost sources to the customer.

Invoice lines must be of two accounting categories:

A) PASS_THROUGH
Examples:
- customs duties
- freight
- port charges
- loading/unloading
- shipping agent costs
- driver advance
- other on-behalf charges

B) REVENUE
Examples:
- customs clearance fee
- administrative fee
- company-owned service fee

Rules:
- Every PASS_THROUGH line must be linked to a cost source allocation
- PASS_THROUGH lines consume available remaining balance from cost sources
- The system must prevent duplicate consumption
- The system must prevent consuming more than the remaining balance
- The invoice is the primary accounting settlement point for pass-through allocation

==================================================
8) INVOICE LIFECYCLE — MANDATORY
==================================================

The system must support two invoice states:

1. DRAFT / PROFORMA
Used when:
- preparing a customer invoice before final confirmation
- sending a preliminary invoice
- reviewing tentative allocations

Rules:
- draft invoice does NOT create journal entries
- draft invoice does NOT affect accounts receivable
- draft invoice does NOT affect VAT payable
- draft invoice does NOT settle cost source balances
- draft invoice remains editable

2. FINAL / POSTED
Used when:
- invoice is confirmed
- allocations are confirmed
- accounting impact must be recorded

Rules:
- final invoice creates journal entries
- final invoice creates customer receivable
- final invoice settles allocated pass-through balances
- final invoice recognizes revenue
- final invoice recognizes VAT where applicable
- final invoice becomes locked against direct editing
- corrections must happen through reversal / adjustment flow, not by editing posted data

==================================================
9) ACCOUNTING ENGINE — MANDATORY
==================================================

I do NOT want a simple ledger with one debit and one credit field.
I want a true journal-based accounting engine.

Required:
- journal_entries
- journal_entry_lines
- sourceType / sourceId
- draft / posted states
- createdBy / postedBy
- postingDate
- fiscalYear
- fiscalPeriod
- sequential numbering (JE-YYYY-NNNN)
- strict validation rules

Rules:
- total debit must equal total credit
- no posting to non-postable parent accounts
- posted entries cannot be edited
- posted entries cannot be deleted
- corrections only through reversal and re-posting logic
- all reports must read from posted journal entries only

10) REQUIRED ACCOUNTING TREATMENT
==================================================

Use this accounting logic:

A) When recording agent charges on credit:
DR On-Behalf Recoverable Costs / Unallocated Pass-Through Costs
CR Agent Payables

B) When paying the shipping agent:
DR Agent Payables
CR Cash / Bank

C) When paying customs duties directly:
DR On-Behalf Recoverable Costs / Unallocated Pass-Through Costs
CR Cash / Bank

D) When paying field advances / driver advances / other recoverable on-behalf costs:
DR On-Behalf Recoverable Costs / Unallocated Pass-Through Costs
CR Cash / Bank

E) When receiving a customer advance:
DR Cash / Bank
CR Customer Advances

F) When posting final invoice:
DR Accounts Receivable — Customer
CR On-Behalf Recoverable Costs / Unallocated Pass-Through Costs (for allocated pass-through share)
CR Clearance Fees Revenue
CR Administrative / Service Revenue (if any)
CR VAT Payable (if applicable)

G) When receiving payment from customer:
DR Cash / Bank
CR Accounts Receivable — Customer

H) When recording true company expenses:
DR Operating Expense
CR Cash / Bank or Payables

I) When making internal treasury/bank transfer:
DR Receiving Cash/Bank
CR Sending Cash/Bank
