==================================================
2) CORE BUSINESS RULE — MANDATORY
==================================================

Pooled recoverable costs are recorded first without assigning them to a single customer by default.

Later, when creating an invoice for a customer:
- the user allocates the exact share belonging to that customer from one or more available cost sources
- the user also adds true company revenue lines such as customs clearance fees and administrative fees
- VAT is calculated only on taxable revenue/service lines
- the final posted invoice creates accounting impact
- draft invoice does not create accounting impact

This is mandatory.
The invoice is the primary settlement point between:
- pooled recoverable costs
- customer receivable
- company revenue
- VAT payable

==================================================
7) INVOICE-TIME ALLOCATION — MANDATORY
==================================================

This is the heart of Phase 4.

When creating an invoice, the user must be able to:
- search/select available cost sources
- view total/allocated/remaining balance for each source
- allocate exact amounts from one or more cost sources to this invoice/customer
- create multiple pass-through lines from those allocations
- combine them with revenue lines such as clearance fees

Required allocation behavior:
- allocation must consume only available remaining cost source balance
- system must prevent over-allocation
- system must prevent duplicate or double consumption
- allocations must persist in a dedicated allocation table/model
- cost source status must update automatically:
  - UNALLOCATED
  - PARTIALLY_ALLOCATED
  - FULLY_ALLOCATED

Important:
Actual allocation is the default logic.
Equal allocation may exist only later as an optional helper, not as the primary workflow.

==================================================
8) DRAFT VS FINAL INVOICE LIFECYCLE — MANDATORY
==================================================

Support these two invoice stages:

A. DRAFT / PROFORMA
Rules:
- draft invoice is fully editable
- draft invoice does NOT create journal entries
- draft invoice does NOT affect accounts receivable
- draft invoice does NOT affect VAT payable
- draft invoice does NOT permanently consume cost source balances
- draft allocations may be temporary/reserved only if architecture supports it safely, otherwise keep allocation finalization only at posting time

B. FINAL / POSTED
Rules:
- posting final invoice validates all allocations
- posting final invoice creates journal entries
- posting final invoice creates customer receivable
- posting final invoice settles allocated pass-through share from recoverable pooled cost account
- posting final invoice recognizes revenue
- posting final invoice recognizes VAT payable when applicable
- posted invoice becomes locked against unsafe editing
- any correction must be handled by controlled correction/reversal flow later, not direct editing

9) ACCOUNTING LOGIC — MANDATORY
==================================================

Implement these posting rules for final posted invoice:

When posting final invoice:

CR On-Behalf Recoverable Costs / Unallocated Pass-Through Costs
(for the total allocated pass-through amount)

CR Clearance Fees Revenue
(for true service fees)

CR Administrative / Other Service Revenue
(for other company revenue lines if any)

CR VAT Payable
(for VAT on taxable revenue lines only)

Important:

- do not apply VAT to pass-through lines
